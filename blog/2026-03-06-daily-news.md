### Iran War Day Six: Clerics Meet to Choose New Supreme Leader as Strikes Continue

![Iran conflict](/img/blog/2026-03-06/iran-conflict.jpg)

The U.S.-Israeli war with Iran enters its sixth day with a pivotal moment: a panel of clerics is meeting to decide on Iran's next supreme leader after **Ayatollah Ali Khamenei** was killed in the opening strikes on February 28. The choice could either cement hardline continuity under his son or usher in a fundamentally transformed regime. President Trump has controversially stated he should be involved in selecting Iran's next leader. ### Spain Refuses US Military Base Access, Trump Threatens Trade Cutoff

![Spain flag](/img/blog/2026-03-06/spain-nato.jpg)

### China Sets Record-Low Growth Target of 4.5–5% at National People's Congress

![China economy](/img/blog/2026-03-06/china-economy.jpg)

China set its **lowest GDP growth target on record** at 4.5% to 5% for 2026, down from "around 5%" in recent years. Premier Li Qiang announced the target at the opening of the **National People's Congress**, acknowledging what Beijing calls a "grave and complex landscape." The country faces persistent deflation, weak consumer confidence, high youth unemployment, and the fallout from Trump's tariffs and escalating trade war.

### "RAMageddon": Global Memory Shortage Drives PC Prices Up 20%+

![Technology chips](/img/blog/2026-03-06/tech-chips.jpg)

The AI-driven **global memory shortage** — dubbed "RAMageddon" — is worsening, with memory prices surging **~90%** in Q1 2026. Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron have pivoted cleanroom capacity toward high-margin **HBM chips** for AI GPUs, starving consumer markets of DRAM and NAND. PC makers Lenovo, Dell, HP, Acer, and ASUS have warned of **15–30% price increases** this year. Analysts expect the shortage to persist beyond 2027.

Sources: [Fast Company](https://www.fastcompany.com/91470364/why-is-there-a-ram-shortage-in-2026-ai-cause-memory-chip-scramble) · [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-15/rampant-ai-demand-for-memory-is-fueling-a-growing-chip-crisis) · [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/rising-memory-prices-pile-more-strain-on-consumer-pc-market)

### Boston Dynamics Atlas Ships to Hyundai and DeepMind — All 2026 Units Committed

![Robotics AI](/img/blog/2026-03-06/robot-hand.jpg)

The production version of Boston Dynamics' **Atlas** humanoid robot — winner of CNET's Best Robot at CES 2026 — has **all deployments fully committed for 2026**. Fleets are shipping to Hyundai's Robotics Metaplant Application Center and **Google DeepMind**. Atlas boasts 56 degrees of freedom, 2.3m reach, and can lift 50kg. ### Google Absorbs Intrinsic to Build "Android for Robots"

Google brought its robotics software unit **Intrinsic** fully in-house from Alphabet's "Other Bets," positioning it to offer manufacturers a **vertically integrated stack**: AI models from DeepMind, deployment software from Intrinsic, and cloud infrastructure from Google Cloud. The move mirrors Google's Android strategy — this time for industrial robots. McKinsey projects the general purpose robot market could reach **$370 billion by 2040**.

### NVIDIA Releases Cosmos and GR00T Models for Physical AI

NVIDIA released new open-source **Cosmos** world models and **GR00T N1.6** vision-language-action models purpose-built for humanoid robots. The **Jetson T4000** module (Blackwell architecture, $1,999) delivers **1,200 FP4 TFLOPS** at just 40–70 watts. Partners including Boston Dynamics, Caterpillar, LG, and NEURA Robotics are already building next-gen robots on the platform — signaling NVIDIA's ambition to be the "Android of generalist robotics."
