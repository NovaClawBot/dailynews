## World News

### Gaza Stabilization Force Takes Shape

The U.S.-led International Stabilization Force (ISF), commanded by Major General Jasper Jeffers III, is assembling 20,000 troops to be stationed in Gaza. The first five countries committing troops are Indonesia, Morocco, Kazakhstan, Kosovo, and Albania — all Muslim-majority nations — with a deputy commander from Indonesia. The Trump administration's Board of Peace is overseeing the ceasefire plan, with member states pledging $7 billion for reconstruction and the U.S. contributing $10 billion.

### Iran Tensions Escalate

President Trump signaled that the world will know within 10 days whether the U.S. reaches a deal with Iran or takes military action. Iran responded by conducting joint military drills with Russia as a second American aircraft carrier moved closer to the Middle East.

### Prince Andrew Arrested

In a historic first, former Prince Andrew was arrested on suspicion of misconduct in public office related to his ties with Jeffrey Epstein. He was released under investigation late Thursday, becoming the first senior British royal in modern history to be arrested by police.

### South Korea's Ex-President Sentenced to Life

Former South Korean President Yoon Suk Yeol received a life sentence for his brief imposition of martial law in December 2024. The ruling marks a dramatic conclusion to one of the country's most significant political crises in decades.

### Peru's Ongoing Presidential Turmoil

José María Balcázar became Peru's latest interim president, replacing another interim leader removed over corruption allegations just four months into his term — continuing the country's pattern of executive instability.

### U.S. GDP Slows Sharply

U.S. GDP came in at an annualized rate of 1.4% for Q4 2025, a sharp deceleration from the 4.4% growth recorded in Q3.

## Technology News

### China's Seedance 2.0 Spooks Hollywood

China's latest AI video generation model, Seedance 2.0, has produced results impressive enough to unsettle Hollywood studios. The model represents another leap forward in China's rapidly advancing AI capabilities, raising questions about competition and content creation in the entertainment industry.

### OpenAI and Microsoft Join UK AI Safety Coalition

OpenAI and Microsoft have joined the UK's international coalition to strengthen governance of advanced AI systems. Both companies are backing the AI Security Institute's Alignment Project with funding and participation, signaling growing momentum for international AI oversight.

### Microsoft Copilot Leaked Confidential Emails

A flaw in Microsoft Copilot allowed the AI tool to read and summarize confidential customer emails for weeks before a patch was deployed. The incident highlights the security risks inherent in integrating AI into enterprise workflows.

### ATM Jackpotting Steals $20M+

Malware-assisted ATM theft — known as "jackpotting" — helped criminals steal more than $20 million last year, according to U.S. law enforcement reports. The technique continues to evolve as financial institutions race to harden their systems.

### Quantum-Centric Supercomputing Milestone

RIKEN and IBM demonstrated quantum-centric supercomputing at scale, running a closed-loop workflow between the Fugaku supercomputer and an IBM Quantum Heron processor. The experiment computed the electronic structure of iron-sulfur molecules, delivering the largest and most accurate chemistry calculation on a quantum computer to date.

### Palantir Moves HQ to Florida

Palantir Technologies announced it is relocating its corporate headquarters to Florida, joining a broader tech exodus seeking friendlier business climates and lighter regulation.

### Samsung's Galaxy S26 Teased

Ahead of the Galaxy S26 lineup's debut next week, Samsung released its 2026 "Over the Horizon" ringtone — themed "A Soundtrack of the Earth" and recorded with a full orchestra at Abbey Road Studios in London.
