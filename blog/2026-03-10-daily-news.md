## World News

### Iran Names Mojtaba Khamenei as New Supreme Leader After Father's Killing

![Iran flag](/img/blog/2026-03-10/iran-leadership.jpg)

Iran's Assembly of Experts has named **Mojtaba Khamenei**, the 56-year-old son of the late Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, as the country's new Supreme Leader. The elder Khamenei was killed in a U.S.-Israeli strike on February 28. Mojtaba's mother, wife, and a sister were also killed in the attack, but he was reportedly not present. IRGC commanders reportedly pressured Assembly members to vote for Mojtaba starting March 3. President Trump responded that the new leader will need "approval from us" or "he's not going to last long," while Israel's military threatened to kill any Khamenei replacement. Russia pledged "unwavering" support and China opposed any targeting of the new leader.

### Oil Briefly Spikes Near $120 as Global Markets Shudder

![Oil markets](/img/blog/2026-03-10/oil-markets.jpg)

Oil prices briefly spiked to nearly **$120 per barrel** — the highest since 2022 — as the Iran war continues to threaten crude supply through the Strait of Hormuz. The spike triggered a global market rout: Japan's Nikkei 225 plunged more than **5%**, South Korea's KOSPI fell **6%**, and the Dow dropped 721 points early Monday. However, U.S. markets staged a dramatic recovery — the S&P 500 flipped from a 1.5% loss to a **0.8% gain**, and oil settled back near $95–$99 per barrel by day's end. CNN called it "the biggest oil disruption in history."

### Seventh U.S. Service Member Killed in Iran War

The Pentagon identified **Sergeant Benjamin N. Pennington**, 26, of Glendale, Kentucky, as the seventh U.S. service member killed in the Iran war. Assigned to the 1st Space Battalion, 1st Space Brigade, Pennington was working at a strategic radar installation for early warning against incoming missile threats in Saudi Arabia when the base was attacked on March 1. He died on March 8 from his injuries. The first six American casualties were Army reservists killed in a March 1 attack on a command center at a Kuwaiti port.

### Israel Escalates Strikes on Lebanon as Hezbollah Conflict Widens

The Israeli military struck **Hezbollah-affiliated financial institutions** in Beirut, including branches of al-Qard al-Hasan in the Bir al-Abed area and near Beirut's international airport. Israeli air raids have killed more than **400 people** and displaced thousands across Lebanon since Hezbollah resumed cross-border attacks on March 2 in response to the killing of Iran's Supreme Leader. The IDF has ordered southern Lebanon residents north, and the Lebanese parliament voted to **extend its own term by two years** due to the escalating crisis. Hundreds of thousands have been displaced.

### Nepal's Ex-Rapper Balendra Shah Set for Landslide Election Victory

![Nepal](/img/blog/2026-03-10/nepal.jpg)

In a historic political shift, **Balendra Shah**, a 35-year-old rapper-turned-politician, is set to become Nepal's next prime minister after his **Rastriya Swatantra Party (RSP)** won **125 out of 165 seats** in the country's first parliamentary election since Gen Z protests ousted the old guard. Shah personally defeated four-time PM Khadga Prasad Sharma Oli in his own constituency. His campaign anthem *Nepal Haseko* ("Nepal Smiling") racked up over 10 million YouTube views during the uprising. The victory unseats Nepal's two long-dominant parties — the Nepali Congress and the Communist Party of Nepal (UML).

### Renewed MH370 Search Ends Without Finding Wreckage

Twelve years after Malaysia Airlines Flight MH370 vanished with **239 people** aboard, a renewed deep-sea search has come up empty. The Air Accident Investigation Bureau confirmed that a search by marine robotics company **Ocean Infinity** between March 2025 and January 2026 surveyed thousands of square kilometers of ocean floor under a "no-find, no-fee" $70 million contract but found no confirmed wreckage. The search vessel has been redeployed for other work and is unlikely to return soon due to approaching winter conditions. Families group Voice 370 urged the Malaysian government to extend the contract.

Sources: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/09/g-s1-112960/12-years-missing-flight-mh370) · [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/renewed-search-for-missing-flight-mh370-comes-up-empty-12-years-later-as-families-press-for-answers) · [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2026/03/08/malaysia-missing-flight-mh370-seabed-search/968d38f4-1abf-11f1-aef0-0aac8e8e94db_story.html)

### Trump Administration Drafts Strict AI Contract Rules, Drops Anthropic

The Trump administration has drawn up new rules requiring AI companies seeking government contracts to grant the U.S. an **irrevocable license for "all lawful" uses** of their models. The policy would also bar vendors from encoding "partisan or ideological judgments" into outputs and require disclosure of compliance with non-U.S. regulatory frameworks. The move follows the Pentagon designating **Anthropic** a "supply-chain risk" and terminating a **$200 million contract** after Anthropic refused to grant unrestricted military use of its technology, citing concerns about surveillance and lethal autonomous weapons.

### Google Reports Record 90 Zero-Day Exploits in 2025, Half Targeting Enterprise

![Cybersecurity](/img/blog/2026-03-10/cybersecurity.jpg)

Google's Threat Intelligence Group tracked **90 zero-day vulnerabilities** exploited in the wild in 2025 — a 15% increase from 2024. Nearly half (**48%**) targeted enterprise software and appliances, both a raw number and proportion all-time high. Commercial surveillance vendors surpassed state-sponsored groups in zero-day exploitation for the first time. China-linked espionage groups remain the most active state actors with 10 exploited zero-days. Microsoft was the most targeted vendor (25 zero-days), followed by Google (11), Apple (8), and Cisco (4).

Sources: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/05/google-says-half-of-all-zero-days-it-tracked-in-2025-targeted-buggy-enterprise-tech/) · [Google Cloud Blog](https://cloud.google.com/blog/topics/threat-intelligence/2025-zero-day-review) · [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/google-half-of-2025s-90-exploited-zero-days-aimed-at-enterprises/)

![Industrial robot](/img/blog/2026-03-10/industrial-robot.jpg)

Google officially brought **Intrinsic** — Alphabet's robotics software "moonshot" — in-house, where it will work closely with **Google DeepMind** and tap into **Gemini AI models** and Google Cloud. ### Boston Dynamics Atlas Enters Production, Targets 30,000 Robots/Year by 2028

At CES 2026, Hyundai and Boston Dynamics unveiled the production version of **Atlas** — a fully electric humanoid standing 6.2 feet tall, weighing 198 pounds, with **56 degrees of freedom** and the ability to lift 110 pounds. The robot won **"Best Robot" at CES 2026**. Boston Dynamics plans to manufacture **30,000 Atlas units per year by 2028** at Hyundai's Metaplant near Savannah, Georgia, where Atlas will help assemble electric vehicles. A new partnership with **Google DeepMind** will integrate foundation models into Atlas for greater cognitive capabilities.

### AI Agent Adoption Jumps From 11% to 42% in Enterprise

Enterprise AI agent adoption has exploded, jumping from **11% to 42%** in just two quarters. According to industry data, 35% of organizations report broad AI agent usage, another 27% are experimenting, and 17% have rolled them out company-wide. Gartner forecasts **80% of enterprise apps** will embed agents by late 2026. The shift marks AI moving from hype to pragmatism — smaller models deployed where they fit, intelligence embedded in physical devices, and systems designed to integrate cleanly into human workflows rather than replace them.
