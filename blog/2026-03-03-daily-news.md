### Oil Surges Past $83 as Hormuz Closure Sparks $100-a-Barrel Forecasts

The shutdown of the Strait of Hormuz sent global energy markets into turmoil. **Brent crude surged to $83.39/barrel** (up 7.3%), while U.S. crude climbed to $76.31 (up 7%). UBS analysts warned Brent could hit **$100 or even $120** per barrel if disruptions persist. European natural gas prices soared **over 70% this week** after Qatar ceased LNG production following Iranian drone attacks on its infrastructure. The U.S. national average gas price spiked **12 cents in a single day** — the largest one-day jump in four years. Analysts warn the worst is yet to come if tanker traffic remains halted.

### NVIDIA Invests $4 Billion in Photonics — Massive Deals with Coherent and Lumentum

![Data center optics](/img/blog/2026-03-03/nvidia-optics.jpg)

NVIDIA announced a combined **$4 billion investment** in photonics companies **Coherent** ($2B) and **Lumentum** ($2B) to secure the supply chain for next-generation AI data centers. Both deals include multibillion-dollar purchase commitments and capacity access rights for advanced laser and optical networking components. The focus: **Co-Packaged Optics (CPO)** technology, which integrates optical transceivers directly into network switches, dramatically reducing costs and energy consumption. Both companies will use the capital to expand U.S.-based manufacturing and R&D — a critical move as AI data center buildouts accelerate globally.

### OpenAI Admits Pentagon Deal Was "Opportunistic and Sloppy" — Renegotiates Terms

OpenAI CEO **Sam Altman** publicly admitted the company's rushed Pentagon deal "looked opportunistic and sloppy" and announced it is **renegotiating terms**. The deal — struck hours after the Trump administration banned rival **Anthropic** from government use and designated it a national security risk — drew fierce backlash. OpenAI has now amended the contract to include language prohibiting **intentional domestic surveillance of U.S. persons**. The Anthropic ban followed a dispute over whether the company could prohibit its AI from being used in mass surveillance or autonomous weapons under a $200M military contract. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth directed the DoD to label Anthropic a "Supply-Chain Risk to National Security."

### Thales Achieves World First: Remote Post-Quantum Security Upgrade for 5G SIMs

**Thales** demonstrated a world-first: remotely deploying **post-quantum cryptography** on SIM and eSIM cards to harden 5G networks against future quantum computing threats — all without replacing devices or interrupting service. Using a "crypto-agile" approach, operators can push quantum-safe encryption algorithms over the air to existing deployed SIMs. The breakthrough signals that the telecom industry can begin quantum-proofing infrastructure now rather than waiting for a costly hardware refresh cycle. Quantum computing threatens to break current encryption, putting mobile communications and critical infrastructure at risk.

Sources: [The Quantum Insider](https://thequantuminsider.com/2026/03/02/thales-remote-post-quantum-5g-sim-upgrade/) · [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/thales-sets-world-first-quantum-070000542.html)

### Boston Dynamics' Atlas Goes Production — Google DeepMind Brains, Hyundai Factories

**Boston Dynamics** is shipping the production version of its all-electric **Atlas humanoid robot** after winning "Best Robot" at CES 2026.
