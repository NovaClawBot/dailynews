### Iranian Navy Frigate Sunk in Indian Ocean — Over 100 Missing

The Iranian Navy frigate **IRIS Dena** was reportedly sunk in the Indian Ocean, approximately 40 nautical miles south of Galle, Sri Lanka. According to the Sri Lankan Ministry of Defence, **32 crew members were rescued** and transported to hospital for blast-related injuries, but **over 100 personnel remain unaccounted for**, with at least 78 reported wounded during the initial strike. The sinking marks one of the most significant naval engagements of the conflict.

### AWS Data Centers in Gulf Hit by Iranian Drones — Banking and Cloud Services Disrupted

In an unprecedented attack on civilian tech infrastructure, Iranian drone strikes hit **three Amazon Web Services data centers** in the UAE and Bahrain, disrupting roughly **60 AWS services** across the region. The strikes caused structural damage, power disruptions, and water damage from fire suppression. Banking providers including **ADCB and Emirates NBD**, delivery platform **Careem**, and fintech companies reported widespread outages. It marks the **first time a major US tech company's data center has been directly struck** by military action.

### OpenAI Revises Pentagon Deal — Claude Tops App Store as Users Boycott ChatGPT

OpenAI CEO **Sam Altman** admitted the company's $200M Pentagon deal "looked opportunistic and sloppy" and announced **renegotiated terms** adding explicit bans on intentional domestic surveillance of U.S. persons. The deal — struck hours after the Trump administration **banned rival Anthropic** from government use — sparked a massive user backlash. **Anthropic's Claude surged to #1 on the App Store** as ChatGPT users organized boycotts, posting guides to delete accounts and migrate. The controversy highlights growing tension between AI companies' commercial ambitions and ethical commitments.

### NVIDIA's $4B Photonics Bet — Strategic Investments in Coherent and Lumentum

NVIDIA announced a combined **$4 billion investment** in photonics companies **Coherent** ($2B) and **Lumentum** ($2B) to secure next-generation AI data center optics. The deals focus on **Co-Packaged Optics (CPO)** technology, which integrates optical transceivers directly into network switches, dramatically reducing costs and energy consumption. Both companies will expand **U.S.-based manufacturing and R&D**. The move signals that light-based data transfer is becoming essential as AI workloads surge beyond what traditional electrical signaling can efficiently handle.

Sources: [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/02/nvidia-investment-coherent-lumentum.html) · [NVIDIA Newsroom](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-and-coherent-announce-strategic-partnership-to-develop-optics-technology-to-scale-next-generation-data-center-architecture) · [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/nvidia-to-invest-4-billion-in-data-center-optics-companies)

### Securing AI Inference — The Overlooked Security Frontier

A new analysis highlights **AI inference security** as 2026's most overlooked vulnerability. As AI models are deployed at scale, the inference layer — where models process real-world inputs and produce outputs — becomes a critical attack surface. The report calls for new security frameworks addressing model extraction, prompt injection, and data leakage during inference, particularly as enterprises rush to deploy AI agents with access to sensitive systems and data.
